About

üë®üèæ‚Äçüéì Hello! I am a Computer Science PhD candidate at UCLA with Prof. Yizhou Sun and Prof. Kai-Wei Chang.

‚òï My work spans machine learning, mathematical theory, social justice, and community organizing. Broadly, I care about just and inclusive approaches to graph machine learning and natural language processing, including social fairness, ethics, and harms. My PhD research focuses on the theoretical foundations of social unfairness in graph neural networks; I am particularly interested in leveraging spectral graph theory and statistics to characterize how structural properties of graphs contribute to algorithmic unfairness. I am further a core organizer of Queer in AI, through which I research how AI impacts queer communities and advocate for LGBTQIA+ rights in AI policy.
